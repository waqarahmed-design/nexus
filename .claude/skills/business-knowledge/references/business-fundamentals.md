# Business Fundamentals

## Business Model Canvas

Map any product across nine building blocks before designing:

| Block | Question | Design impact |
|---|---|---|
| Value Proposition | What unique value do we deliver? | Hero messaging, onboarding copy, landing page |
| Customer Segments | Who are our users? | Persona-driven UX, segmented flows |
| Channels | How do users find us? | Acquisition flows, referral UI, sharing features |
| Customer Relationships | Self-service, personal, automated? | Support UI, onboarding depth, community features |
| Revenue Streams | How do we make money? | Pricing pages, upgrade prompts, payment flows |
| Key Resources | What assets power the product? | Feature availability, performance expectations |
| Key Activities | What must we do well? | Core feature investment, quality thresholds |
| Key Partners | Who do we depend on? | Integration UIs, partner branding, API limits |
| Cost Structure | What are our major costs? | Feature cost awareness, infrastructure constraints |

## Revenue Models

### Model types and their design implications

**Subscription (SaaS)**
- Design: Tier comparison tables, upgrade nudges, usage dashboards, billing management
- Key metric: MRR/ARR, churn rate, expansion revenue
- Design rule: Make the value of higher tiers visible in daily usage

**Freemium**
- Design: Free tier limitations must be visible but not punishing, upgrade path clear
- Key metric: Free-to-paid conversion rate (target 2–5%)
- Design rule: Let users hit the paywall naturally through usage, never artificially

**Marketplace**
- Design: Two-sided trust (reviews, verification), transaction flow, discovery/search
- Key metric: Take rate, GMV, liquidity (supply/demand balance)
- Design rule: Reduce friction in the core transaction above all else

**Advertising**
- Design: Ad placement that doesn't destroy UX, premium ad-free tier
- Key metric: CPM, CTR, ad viewability, time on site
- Design rule: Content quality drives ad revenue — never sacrifice content for ads

**Licensing / Enterprise**
- Design: Admin consoles, team management, SSO, audit logs, custom branding
- Key metric: ACV (Annual Contract Value), seats, expansion
- Design rule: Admin UX is as important as end-user UX

## Unit Economics

### Key metrics every designer should know

**CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)**
- Total sales + marketing spend / new customers acquired
- Design impact: Onboarding completion rate directly affects CAC efficiency
- Target: CAC payback period < 12 months

**LTV (Lifetime Value)**
- Average revenue per user x average customer lifespan
- Design impact: Retention features, engagement loops, and upgrade paths increase LTV
- Healthy ratio: LTV:CAC should be 3:1 or better

**Churn Rate**
- Users lost in a period / total users at start of period
- Design impact: Every friction point, confusing flow, and unmet expectation contributes to churn
- Benchmark: <5% monthly for B2C, <2% monthly for B2B SaaS

**ARPU (Average Revenue Per User)**
- Total revenue / total active users
- Design impact: Feature adoption and upgrade conversion directly increase ARPU

## Market Analysis

### TAM, SAM, SOM

- **TAM (Total Addressable Market)** — Everyone who could theoretically use the product
- **SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market)** — The segment you can reach with your current model
- **SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)** — The realistic share you can capture in 1–3 years

Design implication: SOM defines your actual user base. Design for SOM users, not TAM fantasies.

### Market sizing approach
1. Top-down: Industry reports → segment → your share
2. Bottom-up: Unit price x addressable users x conversion rate
3. Value theory: How much value do you create? What % can you capture?

## Competitive Positioning

### Analysis framework

For each competitor, map:
- **Feature parity** — What do they have that we don't? What do we have that they don't?
- **UX quality** — Where are they excellent? Where are they frustrating?
- **Pricing** — How do they price relative to value delivered?
- **Positioning** — Who do they target? What's their core promise?
- **Gaps** — Where are users underserved across all competitors?

Design implication: Don't copy competitors. Identify gaps and own them.

## Pricing Strategy

### Models

- **Value-based** — Price based on value delivered to customer (preferred)
- **Cost-plus** — Cost of delivery + margin (commodity products)
- **Competitive** — Match or undercut competitors (race to bottom)
- **Penetration** — Low initial price to gain market share, raise later
- **Skimming** — High initial price for early adopters, lower over time

### Pricing page design principles
1. Anchor with the most popular/recommended plan
2. Show 3 tiers maximum (good/better/best)
3. Highlight the plan you want most users to choose
4. Show annual pricing as default (with monthly option)
5. Feature comparison must be scannable, not a wall of checkmarks
6. Include social proof near the pricing decision point
