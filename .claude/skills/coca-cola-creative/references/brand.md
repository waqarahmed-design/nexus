# Coca-Cola Brand Reference

## Brand Purpose
To refresh the world and make a difference. Coca-Cola exists to uplift everyday moments — turning the ordinary into something worth remembering.

## Brand Vision
"Craft the brands and choice of drinks that people love, to refresh them in body and spirit."

## Current Brand Platform: Real Magic
Launched 2021. Built on the belief that humanity's greatest moments happen when we come together — and that Coca-Cola is present in those moments.

**Core Insight:** In a world of increasing division and digital distance, real human connection — however brief — is magical. A shared Coke is a small act with outsized meaning.

**What "Real Magic" means creatively:**
- Magic is not fantasy — it's the feeling of a real moment intensified
- It lives in the in-between: the pause, the glance, the first sip
- It belongs to everyone, everywhere, regardless of culture or background

## Brand Positioning
Coca-Cola is the universal symbol of togetherness, optimism, and the simple pleasure of being alive right now.

**Positioning Statement:** For people everywhere, Coca-Cola is the taste of real human connection — the drink that turns an ordinary moment into something you'll always remember.

## Brand Values
- **Optimism** — See the good in people and the world; lead with joy
- **Togetherness** — Shared moments > individual experiences
- **Authenticity** — Real feelings, real people, real moments — never manufactured
- **Inclusivity** — Everyone gets a Coke. No one is excluded from the magic
- **Simplicity** — The most powerful things are often the most uncomplicated

## Brand Personality
- **Warm** — Like a friend who's always happy to see you
- **Playful** — Light without being frivolous; fun without being childish
- **Confident** — Doesn't need to shout; the brand knows who it is
- **Optimistic** — Not naive, but genuinely believes in the good in people
- **Universal** — Belongs to the world, not to a demographic

## Brand Voice
- Direct and warm — speaks like a person, not a corporation
- Simple words, genuine emotion — no advertising hyperbole
- Inclusive "we" and "you" — the brand is in the moment with you
- Never preachy, never ironic at the expense of warmth
- Short sentences land harder than long ones

**Voice Examples:**
- ✓ "The magic was always real."
- ✓ "One Coke. One moment. That's enough."
- ✗ "Coca-Cola: Refreshing you since 1886." (corporate, cold)
- ✗ "This isn't just a drink. It's a revolution." (overwrought)

## Target Audience
**Primary:** Gen Z and Millennials globally (18–34)
- Deeply values authenticity; allergic to performative brand behavior
- Seeks connection in an increasingly disconnected world
- Experiences joy in small, shared moments
- Globally culturally connected but proudly locally rooted

**Secondary:** Everyone — Coca-Cola is a universal brand; campaigns should feel personally relevant across generations

## Heritage & Equity
- Founded 1886, Atlanta, Georgia
- Available in 200+ countries — one of the most recognised brands on earth
- The contour bottle (1915) and Spencerian script are among the most iconic design assets in history
- Associated with major cultural moments: WWII soldier ads, "I'd Like to Teach the World to Sing" (1971), Mean Joe Greene (1979), "Share a Coke" (2011)
- Santa Claus's modern red-and-white image was popularised by Coca-Cola's 1930s advertising (Haddon Sundblom illustrations)

## What Coca-Cola Is NOT
- Not edgy, cynical, or ironic in a detached way
- Not a status symbol or luxury marker
- Not political (though it can be humanitarian and inclusive)
- Not trying to be cool — it simply is, because everyone drinks it
- Not a health brand, a tech brand, or a performance brand

## The Bottle as Icon
The contour bottle is a character, not just a container. It:
- Signals Coke before the label is read
- Represents accessibility — anyone can hold one
- Visually communicates curves, warmth, and satisfaction
- Should appear in campaigns as a participant in the moment, not a product shot
